UniArchive Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 12, 2025

UniArchive (“we”, “us”, or “our”) provides a mobile application that allows university students and academic staff to store, share, and view exam documents. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, share, and protect your information when you use the UniArchive app (the “Service”).

By using UniArchive you agree to the practices described below. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

  • Email address (required to create and access your account)
  • Password (stored using Supabase Authentication; we never see or store the plain text password)

1.2 Content You Provide

  • Uploaded PDF or other exam documents
  • Metadata about files (university, faculty, course, exam notes, etc.)
  • Comments or additional descriptions attached to resources

1.3 Usage Data

  • Device information (model, OS version) and diagnostics collected by Expo/React Native runtime
  • Log data, request metadata, and error traces
  • Aggregate analytics (search frequency, upload counts, etc.)

1.4 Storage Data

  • Offline copies of limited data stored on your device via AsyncStorage to maintain session state

We do not collect sensitive personal information such as government identifiers, precise location, or financial data.

2. How We Use Information

  1. Create and maintain your UniArchive account
  2. Authenticate and authorize access to exam archives
  3. Store and display educational content you upload
  4. Provide search, filtering, and navigation features
  5. Send essential service messages (verification emails, alerts)
  6. Monitor stability, detect abuse, and prevent fraud
  7. Improve the Service through analytics and development

3. Legal Bases for Processing

  • Performance of a contract to provide UniArchive
  • Legitimate interests (security, improvements, anti-abuse)
  • Consent where required for communications
  • Compliance with legal obligations

4. Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell your personal data. We may share information with:

  • Supabase – authentication, database, and storage provider storing your email, hashed password, metadata, and files.
  • Expo / React Native services – crash reports, diagnostics, OTA updates.
  • Service providers – vetted partners for analytics, error logging, or security (only minimal data as needed).
  • Legal authorities – when required by law or to protect rights and safety.

5. Data Retention

  • Data remains active until you delete it or request account deletion.
  • Backups/logs retained up to 180 days unless longer retention is required.
  • Deleted content is removed from active systems immediately and purged from backups within the retention cycle.

6. Your Rights

You may have the right to:

  • Access a copy of your data
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Delete your data or account
  • Restrict or object to processing
  • Export your data in a portable format

Contact support@uniarchive.app to exercise these rights. We may verify your identity before fulfilling requests.

7. Data Security

  • Encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS)
  • Supabase-managed authentication with hashed passwords
  • Row-Level Security (RLS) for controlled access
  • Least-privilege access for team members

No system is completely secure; keep your credentials confidential and alert us if you suspect unauthorized activity.

8. Children’s Privacy

UniArchive is intended for university-level students and academic staff. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16 (or the minimum age in your jurisdiction). Contact us if you believe a minor has provided data.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be stored on servers outside your country (e.g., Supabase data centers in the EU or US). We rely on contractual safeguards and industry-standard practices to protect data transferred internationally.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy. When changes are material, we will notify you via the app or email. Continued use of UniArchive after updates constitutes acceptance.
